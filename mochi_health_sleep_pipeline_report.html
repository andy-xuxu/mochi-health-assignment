01 · Executive Summary

The Orexin Era Is Here.
Mochi Has a Window.

A paradigm shift is underway in sleep and wakefulness medicine. For three decades, treatment for narcolepsy and related disorders relied on symptomatic management — stimulants that keep patients awake without addressing what's wrong. That era is ending. Orexin receptor agonists, which replace the brain's missing wake signal at its source, are entering the market for the first time.

This presents a concrete opportunity for Mochi Health. As a telehealth platform with existing infrastructure for medication management, prior authorization support, compounding pharmacy relationships, and a patient base that skews toward metabolic and energy-related conditions, Mochi is better positioned than legacy care to capture this new patient category — if it moves early.

02 · Market Context

Why Sleep & Energy, Why Now

The success of GLP-1 therapeutics has done more than create a weight loss category — it has changed how the pharmaceutical industry thinks about receptor-level biology. The same investigator networks, FDA pathways, and investor appetite that accelerated GLP-1 development are now being applied to orexin, a neuropeptide system governing wakefulness, metabolism, mood, and attention.

Three forces are converging to make this moment strategically significant:

1. First disease-modifying drug for narcolepsy is weeks away from FDA approval

Oveporexton (Takeda's TAK-861), an oral OX2R agonist, received FDA priority review with a PDUFA date in Q3 2026. Two Phase 3 trials — FirstLight and RadiantLight — met all primary and secondary endpoints in July 2025. This will be the first drug approved to address the underlying orexin deficiency rather than just manage symptoms. The launch will generate significant patient and prescriber attention.

2. Chronic insomnia treatments have quietly improved

A newer class of sleep aids — dual orexin receptor antagonists (DORAs) — offer meaningfully better safety profiles than benzodiazepines or Z-drugs. Daridorexant (Quviviq) and lemborexant (Dayvigo) are FDA-approved, non-scheduled, and telehealth-prescribable. Yet adoption lags because most patients still get zolpidem by default.

3. Energy peptides are a growing patient demand with regulatory ambiguity

Compounds like BPC-157, MOTS-c, and CJC-1295 are widely sought by patients but lack FDA approval and rigorous human trial data. This is simultaneously a risk (compounding restrictions) and an opportunity (Mochi can be the credible, evidence-guided voice in a noisy space).

03 · Narcolepsy Therapeutics — Clinical Review

From Symptom Management to Disease Modification

Narcolepsy type 1 (NT1) is caused by autoimmune destruction of orexin-producing neurons in the lateral hypothalamus. NT1 patients have CSF orexin levels below 110 pg/mL — often undetectable — and the HLA-DQB1*06:02 allele. The hallmark symptoms are excessive daytime sleepiness (EDS) and cataplexy (sudden muscle weakness triggered by emotion), along with sleep paralysis, hypnagogic hallucinations, and disrupted nighttime sleep.

Narcolepsy type 2 (NT2) and idiopathic hypersomnia (IH) are related conditions with overlapping EDS presentation but without orexin deficiency or cataplexy. An estimated 135,000–200,000 Americans have narcolepsy, with average diagnostic delay exceeding 10 years.

04 · Insomnia & Sleep Disorders — Clinical Review

The DORA Generation: Safer Sleep That's Underutilized

Chronic insomnia disorder (CID) affects an estimated 30% of U.S. adults, with 10% meeting criteria for clinical insomnia. The insomnia therapeutics market was valued at $6.8 billion in 2025. Yet despite this scale, most patients still receive zolpidem (a Z-drug / GABA-A agonist) with significant next-day impairment, dependency risk, and contraindications in elderly patients.

Dual orexin receptor antagonists (DORAs) represent a mechanistically superior class for insomnia: rather than sedating the brain broadly, they block orexin's wake-promoting signal, allowing natural sleep to occur. The result is better sleep architecture, reduced dependency risk, and improved next-day functioning.

FDA Approved · 2022
Daridorexant
Quviviq · Idorsia Pharmaceuticals

Phase 3 trials (NCT03545191, NCT03575104) demonstrated significant improvement in sleep onset, WASO, and subjective sleep quality at 3 months. 50mg dose showed consistent improvements in sleep architecture without suppressing REM or delta sleep. No next-day impairment; non-scheduled.

Available in 5mg and 10mg. Eisai's established DORA platform also drives their orexin agonist work (E2086).

05 · Energy & Fatigue Peptides — Pipeline Status

High Demand, Thin Evidence, Real Risk

Unlike narcolepsy and insomnia drugs — which have robust Phase 3 trial data and clear FDA pathways — the "energy peptide" category is characterized by significant patient demand, limited rigorous human data, and active regulatory scrutiny. This is the highest-risk section of the landscape, but also the most relevant to Mochi's existing patient population.

⚠️
Regulatory Reality Check BPC-157 and MOTS-c are not FDA-approved for any indication. BPC-157 is classified as an FDA Category 2 bulk drug substance (cannot be compounded by commercial pharmacies). Only 3 published human studies exist for BPC-157 as of early 2026 — all small pilot studies without placebo controls. The landscape of "peptide wellness" that patients encounter online is largely unregulated and carries product quality and safety risks.
No Human Phase Trials Complete
BPC-157
Body Protection Compound-157 · Various

Pentadecapeptide isolated from gastric juice. Extensive animal data for tissue repair, anti-inflammatory effects, GI healing. Promising preclinical profile, but only ~30 humans studied across 3 published trials (all small, no controls). FDA Category 2 (2023): cannot be compounded. The gray market is widespread. A 2015 Phase 1 trial (NCT02637284, 42 volunteers) was canceled without publishing results.

09 · Risks & Considerations

What Could Go Wrong

Oveporexton FDA Delay
Priority review doesn't guarantee approval on PDUFA date. If FDA requests additional data (Complete Response Letter), market launch could shift to 2027. Mochi's pre-launch positioning work is not wasted, but patient communication and prescriber readiness programs should be designed to flex with timing uncertainty.
Medium
Controlled Substance Prescribing
Sodium oxybate (Schedule III) requires a REMS program that is difficult for standard telehealth workflows. Most DORAs (Schedule IV) are manageable, but prescribers must conduct appropriate evaluation before prescribing any scheduled substance. Mochi needs clinical protocol documentation for each drug class.
Medium
Peptide Compounding Crackdown
Following the GLP-1 compounding enforcement, the FDA has signaled continued attention to unapproved peptide compounding. Ipamorelin and CJC-1295 may face Category 2 classification in the near term. Any Mochi offerings in this space should be reviewed by regulatory counsel before launch and maintained as a second-tier offering.
High
Insurance Coverage Gaps
Newer drugs like pitolisant (Wakix) can exceed $500/month without coverage. Narcolepsy payers often require documented MSLT or polysomnography results, which are difficult to obtain via telehealth alone. Mochi would need lab and sleep study referral partnerships to facilitate prior authorizations for the most diagnostic-intensive prescriptions.
Medium
Misdiagnosis Risk
Narcolepsy is frequently misdiagnosed as depression, ADHD, or general fatigue. A telehealth-first model must include validated screening tools (ESS, STOP-BANG, Swiss Narcolepsy Scale) and clear escalation criteria to in-person sleep specialists. Prescribing stimulants or wake-promoting agents to patients with undiagnosed sleep apnea is clinically dangerous.
High
Competitive Moat
Hims & Hers, Done, and emerging sleep-specific telehealth platforms could move quickly into this space. Mochi's advantage is its established infrastructure, board-certified provider network, and track record with complex medication management (GLP-1 compounding). Speed of category entry will determine defensibility.
Low-Med
